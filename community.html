Diversity and Inclusion at JMU

JMU continually embodies a standard for acceptance, diversity, and inclusion. We strive to create a campus where everyone feels welcome and able to achieve. Not only are we proud of our student base, but we also provide clubs and organizations for minorities to thrive.

Statistics surrounding the JMU community:
JMU's student base is made up of 74.7% White, 6.74% Hispanic or Latino, 4.95% Black or African American, 4.63% Two or More Races, 4.58% Asian, 0.11% American Indian or Alaska Native, and 0.101% Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islanders.
We provide a multitude of clubs and organizations for minorities to thrive, including the African Student Organization, Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Asian Student Union, Black Student Alliance, Brothers of a New Direction, BIOSOC: Biology Students of Color, and many more!
In addition to our diverse community, and plethora of organizations, we teach our students how to understand and accept others. Some of the ways we do this is through Implicit Bias Teachings, and Freshman Orientation Guide education.
Here at JMU we continue to emphasize Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion. We not only uphold these values, but we continually strive to keep our campus updated and educated surrounding these matters.
